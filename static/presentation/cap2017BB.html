CAP Conference 2017, June 15-17

Research question

What factors drive the executive agenda between elections?

-> Party mandate

-> Responsiveness to real-world problems as signalled by the media

Study of executive agendas

  • Policy output (legislative, budget)
  • Symbolic agendas (Queen's/Prime Minister speeches)
  • Decisions of the weekly Council of Ministers (through an analysis of press releases)

Dependent variable: Council of minister agenda

  • CoM decisions include:
    • adopting new legislation (either by adopting a bill to be submitted to Parliament, by using executive decrees or asking authorizations to legislate to the Parliament)
    • filling in the details of existing legislation through secondary measures
    • creating new agencies
    • signing international treaties
    • appointing officials

Hypotheses on the Portuguese case:

  • H1. Both the media and the electoral mandate as expressed in the majority platform are expected to influence the CoM agenda, but government platform agenda should have a stronger effect than that of media agenda
  • H2. The main opposition parties' platform has a stronger influence on the CoM agenda than the challenger opposition platform
  • H3. The influence of the majority platform on the CoM agenda is expected to increase in times of economic crisis

Data

  • Period: Portugal from 2002 to 2015 (4 legislatures)
  • Executive agendas: 669 weekly press releases containing a total of 7419 decisions
  • Party platforms: pledges included in each party platform of 5 major parties (Socialists and Social Democrats+Conservatives alternated in government, whereas Comunists and Left Bloc had not supported any cabinet until 2015)
  • Economy index: overall unemployment rate + 10-year government bond yields (normalised because of different units of analysis)

Interaction effect between attention in government platform and economy

Preliminary findings

  • Both the media and the electoral mandate influence the CoM agenda, but government platform agenda does have a stronger effect than the media agenda (H1)
  • Type of opposition matters: main opposition parties' platform has a stronger influence than the challenger opposition platform (H2)
  • The influence of the majority platform on the CoM agenda increases in times of economic crisis (H3)
